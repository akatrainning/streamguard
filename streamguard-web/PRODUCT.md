# Product

## Register

product

## Users

StreamGuard primarily serves regulatory and compliance reviewers who need to inspect live commerce streams with evidence, consistency, and speed. Secondary users include live operations staff who need real-time intervention cues, and consumer-decision users who compare risky live rooms before taking action.

The primary usage context is task-focused: a reviewer or operator sits in front of a desktop interface, monitors live-room signals, checks suspicious claims, and preserves evidence for later review. They need confidence, not entertainment.

## Product Purpose

StreamGuard is a live commerce risk monitoring cockpit. It connects to live rooms, watches speech and chat signals, identifies risky claims, surfaces rationality and compliance signals, and preserves evidence for post-session review.

Success means a reviewer can quickly answer three questions: what is happening, why it is risky, and what evidence supports that judgment.

## Brand Personality

Cold-headed, professional, trustworthy, and technically sharp.

The product should feel like a mature risk-control or monitoring system, not a marketing site, entertainment dashboard, or coursework demo. It should communicate operational authority through disciplined layout, restrained copy, precise data states, and purposeful motion.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI dashboards with purple gradients, vague intelligence claims, and decorative cards.
- Coursework-style pages with centered text blocks, loose alignment, and no product hierarchy.
- Over-rounded SaaS surfaces that make a monitoring tool feel toy-like.
- Loud marketing pages where visual flourish obscures operational state.
- Identical card grids where every item has the same weight, padding, and rhythm.

## Design Principles

- Compliance first: prioritize evidence, traceability, risk level, and decision support.
- Operational clarity beats decoration: every surface should help users decide or verify.
- Dense data is acceptable when grouping, hierarchy, and rhythm are clear.
- State must be obvious: connected, scanning, warning, high risk, empty, locked, and saved.
- Motion should communicate live signal or state change, not perform for its own sake.
- Design impact matters: visual sophistication is important, but it must reinforce product trust.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG AA target is required for this project stage. Still keep pragmatic usability: keyboard-operable primary controls, distinguishable status states beyond color alone where feasible, readable contrast for core content, and reduced-motion awareness for heavy animations.

When design impact and strict accessibility conflict, prioritize design impact unless the issue blocks basic use.
